⏳ TimeMachine

A live-style tracker for the wild mammals moving through San Francisco, centered on SHACK15 at the Ferry Building. Inspired by the OCEARCH shark tracker — but for the coyotes, raccoons, foxes, skunks and deer that share the city.

Five collared animals, three weeks of fixes. Drag the timeline or hit play to travel through time and replay every track, or go LIVE to watch fresh fixes arrive in real time.

How to use it

  • Click an animal to open its profile — distance walked, average & top speed — and zoom to its range.
  • Filter by species, sex, life stage or transmitter status.
  • Scrub the time machine to replay the last three weeks.
  • Toggle LIVE for a real-time fix stream; switch basemaps (streets / light / terrain / satellite).
  • Hit Ask Claude on any profile for plain-English field notes.

Species, locations and home-range behaviour are real and documented for the Bay Area. Individual animals, names, collar programs, dates and GPS fixes are synthetic — this is a demonstration, not a biological data source. Maps © OpenStreetMap contributors, CARTO, Esri & OpenTopoMap.
